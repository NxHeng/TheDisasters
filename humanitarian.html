WHAT'S HUMANITARIAN CRISIS?

A humanitarian crisis is a singular event or a series of events that are threatening in terms of health, safety or well-being of a community or large group of people. This is a situation where large numbers of people are unable to access basic necessities such as food, water, shelter, and medical care due to factors such as conflict, natural disasters, or displacement. Such crises often result in widespread suffering, malnutrition, disease, and death. They can also lead to the breakdown of social and economic systems, displacement of populations, and violations of human rights. Humanitarian organizations and governments often respond by providing emergency aid and assistance, but addressing the root causes of the crisis is crucial to achieving long-term solutions and preventing future crises.

Effects of Humanitarian Disaster On Humans

The loss of homes due to humanitarian crisis is a significant problem that affects millions of people worldwide. When people lose their homes, they are forced to leave behind their belongings, memories, and sometimes their loved ones.

The loss of people lives in war can have profound effects on individuals and families, such as grief, depression, anxiety, and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD).

The large numbers of people being displaced from their homes, and many of them may become refugees. Refugees are people who have fled their home country due to persecution, war, or violence and are unable or unwilling to return.

The most pressing challenges during humanitarian crisi is that people may face is food shortage. When people do not have access to enough food, it can lead to malnutrition, starvation, and even death.
