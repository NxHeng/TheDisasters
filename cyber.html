Cyber Disasters

A cyber disasters is any attempt to gain unauthorized access to a computer, computing system or computer network with the intent to cause damage. Cyber attacks aim to disable, disrupt, destroy or control computer systems or to alter, block, delete, manipulate or steal the data held within these systems. Any individual or group can launch a cyber attack from anywhere by using one or more various attack strategies.

Type of Cyber Disasters

Data Breaches

Unauthorized access to sensitive information, causing severe impact including financial losses, reputational damage, and legal consequences.

Ransomware Attacks

Malware that encrypts files and demands payment in exchange for decryption, leading to disruption of critical services, financial losses, and reputational damage.

Insider Threats

Authorized individuals using their access maliciously, leading to financial losses, reputational damage, and legal consequences

Why Happen Cyber Disasters?

Human Error

Mistakes made by employees, contractors, or vendors, such as misconfigurations, failure to follow security procedures, or falling for phishing scams.

Software Vulnerabilities

Flaws in software code that can be exploited by cybercriminals to gain unauthorized access, steal data, or disrupt operations.

Lack of Security Awareness

A lack of understanding of the importance of cybersecurity and failure to follow best practices can leave organizations vulnerable to cyber attacks.

Example of Cyber Disaster

Equifax data breach

One of the three major credit reporting agencies in the United States, and in July 2017, hackers gained access to sensitive personal information of approximately 143 million Equifax customers, including names, Social Security numbers, birth dates, addresses, and in some cases, driver's license numbers.
